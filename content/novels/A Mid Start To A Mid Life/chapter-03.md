The walk to Smithing Peak was, as expected, completely uneventful.

We crossed a rather elegant stone bridge spanning the river that flowed beneath the peak. Flowers bloomed along both sides of the path, ancient trees cast cool shadows over the polished stone walkways, and carefully maintained gardens stretched out in every direction.

It was beautiful.

It was peaceful.

It was...

...filled with girls.

You know, Zhou actually had a point.

There were a lot of beautiful women around.

Cultivation was basically free plastic surgery. Every breakthrough polished away imperfections, refined the body, and somehow made everyone look like they belonged on a magazine cover.

Seriously.

The average disciple here would be considered ridiculously attractive back on Earth.

As we walked, Zhou and I talked about absolutely nothing.

Apparently Elder Liu from Alchemy Peak had finally broken through his bottleneck.

Some artifact refiner had created a new spirit sword that caught the managers' attention.

Someone else had improved a talisman formation.

Normal cultivation gossip.

Oddly enough, it sounded less like immortal cultivators discussing the mysteries of heaven and more like office workers talking around the coffee machine.

I had expected a cultivation world.

Instead, I got corporate gossip with spirit stones.

Eventually we arrived at the lecture hall.

The building looked just as majestic as the first time I'd seen it.

Towering jade pillars.

Elegant architecture.

Massive statues of legendary sword cultivators.

Everything about it screamed _prestige_.

Disciples sat in neat rows before the elder's seat, chatting amicably while waiting for the lecture to begin.

Someone waved at us from the middle rows.

I looked over.

Ah.

Our clique.

Three girls.

One guy.

And now the two of us.

The most aggressively average friend group in the sect.

They had even saved seats for us.

The joys of having friends.

"Hi, Zhou!"

"Hello, Xu!"

The cheerful greeting came from... let's just call her **Placeholder Name** (author: No ideas right now) for now.

She was probably my closest friend in the sect.

We had entered together years ago, and our cultivation residences were only a few streets apart.

She was pretty.

Then again...

Everyone here was pretty.

"Morning."

I sat down beside her while Zhou took the remaining seat.

The usual greetings followed.

How've you been?

Anything new?

Still alive?

The standard conversation.

Then—

**BOOM.**

The elder arrived.

Not by walking through the door like a normal person.

No.

An explosion of colorful spiritual light illuminated the sky above the lecture hall. Brilliant auroras danced overhead as the elder descended from the heavens with enough flair to announce to the entire sect:

_"I have reached a realm where entrances matter more than efficiency."_

Honestly...

High-level cultivators were so damn cool.

The lecture began.

The elder explained the proper circulation of qi through the meridians.

_"Qi should flow like a river..."_

Something about steady currents.

Natural cycles.

Harmony between heaven and earth.

Cultivators nodded thoughtfully.

A few disciples practiced on the spot.

One or two particularly talented members of the main lineage even broke through during the lecture.

Typical cultivation stuff.

Eventually the elder began answering questions.

While everyone else paid attention...

I felt something brush against my divine sense.

A tiny thread of spiritual qi.

Encoded within it was a string of runes.

Basically a communication signature.

The cultivation world's equivalent of someone handing you their phone number.

...

Why?

More importantly...

Who?

No way.

Was this finally it?

Was the romance arc beginning?

Had fate finally remembered I existed?

Was the world's overwhelming mediocrity finally cracking apart?

...

No.

Of course not.

I traced the spiritual thread back to its source.

A young woman around our age.

I recognized her.

She worked under Internal Affairs—the department responsible for disciple housing, food, and other logistics.

She looked good.

Not "peerless beauty that shakes the heavens" good.

Our sect didn't have those.

Just...

attractive.

Actually...

She was pretty close to my type.

She noticed me looking.

A tiny smile appeared on her lips.

Then...

She winked.

...

Brother.

My heart skipped exactly one beat.

I discreetly glanced around.

Thankfully none of my friends had noticed.

I took out my communication jade, entered the rune signature, and opened a chat.

**???:** Can I ask if your friend is single?

What?

**Xu Huang:** Which friend?

**???:** The one sitting to your left.
