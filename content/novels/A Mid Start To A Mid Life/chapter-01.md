Am I a cultivator?

That has been my main question for the past twenty days.

Generally speaking, when transmigrators arrive in a new world, they figure things out within two or three days. They recognize the tropes, identify the protagonists, and immediately start making plans.

You know the type.

_"That guy has main-character aura. I should either avoid him or become his best friend."_

Or maybe they discover they're actually a cultivation cauldron for some terrifying demonic expert who just so happens to be a breathtakingly beautiful woman.

Me?

Nothing.

Absolutely nothing.

I'm average among the average. The definition of mediocre. The very peak of being completely unremarkable.

So, who am I?

My name is Xu Huang, an outer disciple of the Green Leaf Sword Sect.

I work as a clerk for the Finance Division under the External Affairs Peak.

Sounds important, doesn't it?

It isn't.

I don't even do real bookkeeping. People hand me money, I write the amount in a notebook, and at the end of the day I report everything to my supervisor. He checks that I haven't skimmed a few spirit stones off the top, nods, and sends me home.

Thrilling work.

As for family...

The only relative I have left is my older sister. She's a cultivator and married to a fairly well-off disciple from the neighboring Smithing Peak—the one that produces artifacts.

Her husband works in logistics.

I think.

He spends all day counting iron ingots, recording deliveries, and moving supplies around. Honestly, I have no idea what his actual job is.

They have a daughter who's around nineteen. She's doing much better than I am. Between having two cultivator parents and actual talent, she's never lacked cultivation resources.

Good for her.

Now, friends.

I'm part of a rather famous clique.

Not because we're geniuses.

Not because we're powerful.

We're famous for being spectacularly average.

There are six of us—three boys and three girls—and every single one is painfully ordinary.

We're also known for having absolutely no interest in romance.

Think of us as a social club for people who enjoy having friends but have no desire for dramatic relationships or emotional complications.

Honestly, it suits me just fine. I was like that back on Earth too.

As for the world...

Brother, just imagine the most standard cultivation setting you've ever read.

If you've finished even one cultivation novel, you already know ninety percent of this world.

Spirit stones.

Sect politics.

Young masters.

Ancient inheritances.

Mystical beasts.

The usual.

Still, for the sake of completeness...

This is the Green Leaf Sword Sect.

The sect is divided into several peaks.

Three are administrative, while the others focus on production, security, combat, and, for some reason, an entire peak dedicated solely to war.

The three administrative peaks handle Finance, Internal Affairs, and Foreign Affairs respectively.

And I?

I sit in the Finance Division, writing numbers into a notebook and wondering if I'm actually living in a cultivation novel... or if I'm just an accountant wearing robes.
