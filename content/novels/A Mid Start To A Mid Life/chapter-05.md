It was another average day.

After the disaster that was the party, nothing particularly interesting happened. I did my job, got paid, and split my wages like always. Half went toward cultivation resources. The other half disappeared into daily necessities and transportation fees.

Yes, transportation.

I don't think I've mentioned that before.

When we say "peaks," we're not talking about a single mountain with a fancy building on top. They're actually small mountain ranges. Several mountains grouped together, connected by valleys filled with halls, residences, workshops, warehouses, and whatever facilities that particular division needs.

Each peak is basically its own little district.

Mine—the External Affairs Peak, Finance Division, Mortal World Businesses Department—is located on one of the outer mountains of our peak. Yes, not exactly prestigious.

Now, if you want to visit another peak or deliver supplies, you have two options.

If you're in the Foundation Establishment Realm, congratulations. You get to fly around on your sword like every respectable cultivator.

If you're not...

You ride the transport shuttle.

They're surprisingly reliable. It takes about thirty to forty minutes to travel from my peak to Internal Affairs.

Placeholder works there, by the way.

And lives there.

Anyway, life continued as usual. I made my money, cultivated, slept, repeated.

Then I noticed something in today's newspaper.

Apparently, one of the border regions of the central lands had been attacked.

According to the report, a force from the Demonic Union assaulted an area containing several spirit stone mines. Those mines are pretty important to the Golden Talisman Sect, so naturally everyone is making a big deal out of it.

There have apparently been tensions and small skirmishes between the two sides for years.

Now, whether it was actually the Demonic Union that attacked...

Who knows?

Maybe it was one of their member sects acting on its own.

Maybe it was officially approved.

Maybe it wasn't.

Governments love propaganda, and cultivation sects aren't exactly famous for honest reporting. Everyone tells half the story if it makes them look better.

So all I really know is that something happened.

And that's enough to become my problem.

You see, despite its peaceful-sounding name, the Green Leaf Sect isn't exactly peaceful.

It's still a cultivation sect.

Cultivation sects solve an alarming number of problems by hitting each other with spiritual techniques.

Whenever conflicts break out, the War Peak starts preparing supplies and sending disciples to support the Orthodox Alliance.

Some become scouts.

Some patrol the borders.

Some become logistics personnel.

And some become what people politely call "reinforcements."

History tends to call them cannon fodder.

One of the elders mentioned there's a good chance we'll need to prepare another shipment of supplies soon. There's also a possibility the sect will begin mobilizing more disciples if the conflict continues.

That's...

Concerning.

Not for present me.

For future me.

Wars in cultivation worlds rarely happen overnight.

They simmer.

Small skirmishes become border conflicts.

Border conflicts become campaigns.

Campaigns become wars that somehow last ten or twenty years.

Hopefully, the politicians—or sect leaders, or patriarchs, or whatever title they decide sounds the most intimidating—manage to negotiate a peace agreement before that happens.

I honestly have no idea how the political landscape of the Orthodox Alliance works.

And I'd like to keep it that way.

All I know is that I really, really hope I don't get conscripted.

That would be a cliché too, wouldn't it?

...Oh no.
