Something unexpected caught my attention.

There isn't a global information network.

No newspaper shared by every major sect.

No independent media.

No "Orthodox Alliance Daily."

Nothing.

The best you can get is your local newspaper, and that's about it.

So, if you actually want to know what's happening in the world, your best source of information is that one old guy at work.

You know the one.

He somehow reads every newspaper, knows everyone worth knowing, and can turn a five-minute lunch break into a two-hour political analysis.

Every workplace has one.

Ours did too.

Well... had.

He got transferred to another division.

Fortunately, I could still find him.

So I did.

"Brother," I asked, "what's actually going on with the border?"

He laughed.

Apparently, that region has been contested for generations between the Golden Talisman Sect and the Crimson Devils of the Xihuan Desert.

The reason?

An oasis.

Inside that oasis grows a special lotus that significantly improves cultivation. Even the spiritual pool where the lotus grows is considered a valuable cultivation resource.

Naturally, neither side wants to share.

So they've been trying to take complete control of it for...

Well...

Longer than anyone can remember.

According to him, this isn't even news.

If you found a newspaper from a hundred and twenty years ago, you'd probably see the exact same headline.

Every fifty or sixty years, someone new ends up in charge, decides the previous generation handled things poorly, and starts another round of fighting.

Cultivators really do live long enough to repeat history.

Then I asked him about the thing that actually worried me.

Conscription.

He waved his hand dismissively.

"Relax," he said. "The sect doesn't send just anyone."

According to him, the disciples sent to the front are usually those whose cultivation depends on constant combat, or those who don't have much of a future anyway.

People like me?

Desk workers?

Finance disciples?

We're actually too useful.

Not because we're important.

Because we're reserved.

"In case the sect itself is attacked," he explained, "you'll be conscripted then."

"...Then?"

"You'll buy time."

Buy time.

In other words...

Cannon fodder.

The elite disciples, elders, and the sect's important assets would retreat while the rest of us delayed the enemy.

Apparently, it had happened before.

More than once.

Naturally, I asked the obvious question.

"Then why does anyone join a cultivation sect?"

He shrugged.

"That's just how cultivation works, brother."

"If you don't like it, stop cultivating."

"Go back to the mortal world."

"And spend the rest of your life dealing with imperial officials, taxes, investigations, and whatever the Imperial Family feels like doing."

...

Fair enough.

It was surprisingly educational.

Anyway...

I finally broke through.

I'm now at the Seventh Layer of Qi Gathering.

Not bad.

Only the Eighth, Ninth, and Tenth Layers remain.

After that comes Foundation Establishment.

And that's where the real problem begins.

It's expensive.

Very expensive.

So I've started saving money.

I stopped making unnecessary trips to other peaks.

I've been taking every odd job I can find.

Extra bounties.

Temporary assignments.

Submission handling.

Anything that pays a few extra spirit stones.

Hopefully, by the time I reach the Tenth Layer, I'll have enough to buy a decent Foundation Establishment Pill.

Maybe...

Just maybe...

I'll be the next person throwing a breakthrough celebration.

...

Oh no.

What if it ends exactly like the last one?

What if two idiots start fighting over the same girl at my party?

...

Actually...  
Let's not think about that.

Placeholder was sprinting toward me as fast as she could.  
"Hey, Placeholder," I said. "What's wrong?"

Ding.  
Ding.  
Ding.

Once.  
Twice.  
Three times.  
...  
Nine times.  
Every disciple froze.  
Nine bell chimes.  
That wasn't a ceremony.  
That wasn't a breakthrough celebration.  
That meant the sect was making an emergency announcement.  
A deep, amplified voice spread throughout every peak.

**"Attention, all disciples of the Green Leaf Sect."  
"Our ally, the Golden Talisman Sect, has formally requested military support."  
"The Crimson Devils have declared war and are expected to begin their campaign against the Golden Talisman Sect within one month."  
"The Green Leaf Sect hereby calls for volunteers to reinforce our ally."  
"Those who answer the call shall be rewarded according to merit and their contributions shall be recorded by the sect."  
"Interested disciples may report to their respective halls immediately."**

Well.  
That newspaper aged remarkably well.
